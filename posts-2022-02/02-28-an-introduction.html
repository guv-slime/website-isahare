The First Blog of a Hare Named Emory

By: Emory Hare February 28, 2022

The name's Emory Hare, and my username? Well…it's just my first name plus “is a” followed by my last name. I'm literally a Hare. It's not that great of a username, but I've used it forever, and it kinda suits me. Anyway, let's move past my name / username and get into the nitty gritty of what this blog is all about…me.

My mascot / logo - the "Bun"

emory.About()

I am a 26 y/o artist man. I have struggled with my physical and mental health for much of my adult life, and I want to share some of my experiences here because for the first time in many years, I feel like things are getting better for me.

My anxiety and depression have been with me since childhood but really ramped up during my college years. My inability to hold down a 9 to 5 job after graduating has persisted for years, but thankfully, I can say that I have found the work-life balance that works for me. I am able to get things done by a certain date, but I'm not always able to start and end at the same time each day. I've realized how important that flexibility is for me.

I am an aspiring artist, and I have been drawing since I was a young child. Apparently, I drew a three-dimensional cup at 3 years old and blew my family's minds.

At age 5, I was gaming on a purple gameboy color and PC (Windows 98 FTW). Gaming has shaped my way of thinking, my taste in art, and my love of puzzles. In a way, it is what pushed me to major in computer science.

After college, I bounced from different jobs, but I've recently decided to start my own freelancing / design business. Digital art is one of the three pillars of my freelance design business. The other pillars are graphic design and web design.

I also live stream on Twitch, which is now part of my daily work life. I am able to show my work to the world as I make it.

this.Blog()

This blog will be about anything that I find interesting or worth sharing. There are many things, but I will list the gist of what I plan to write about:

  • Digital Art (software, tools, my workflow, my growth as an artist, individual pieces)

  • Live-streaming (art, development, gaming)

  • Programming / Web Development (my journey, helpful tips for other beginners, software, explanations that are easy to understand, the best resources for help)

  • Reviews for various things that I find useful or interesting

  • Software

  • Apps

  • Tools

  • Games

  • Movies

  • Books

  • Gizmos & Gadgets

  • Original comics or Clog Posts

I plan to post weekly to start. The posts may end up more like journal entries if I motivate myself to do it everyday. I would like to have thoughtful posts or helpful guides once a week though.

I livestream Monday through Friday and post to instagram about once a week. I tweet pretty regularly, so Twitter is probably the best place to go if you want to know when a new blog post is available.

I am blogging to develop my voice as a writer. I want to see if what I put down on paper is how I feel. I also want to help people who are in a similar situation or want to create things.

this.Conclusion()

So that pretty much wraps up the first post. Was it good? Was it interesting? I don't know how to add a commenting section to this blog, so tweet at me and let me know if I do GOOD.

This post will be buried at the bottom of my iframe on my blog page eventually, but it's the start of something new. An all HTML/CSS blog is all I can make right now, but we will see how far I can take it.

Thanks for reading!

~ Emory
